The first district-level fiscal study ever conducted in Costa Rica.

You live in one of the most economically productive districts in Costa Rica. Nosara's economy generates an estimated $2.13 billion USD per year; driven by tourism, real estate, construction, and services. But the fiscal system was never designed to measure, collect, or return resources at the district level. So Nosara contributes hundreds of millions in potential tax revenue, and gets almost nothing back. This study; built on 13+ official data sources and four independent GDP estimation methods; puts the numbers on the table for the first time.

What did we find?

📊GDP validated four ways. Electricity consumption, tourism revenue, construction activity, and fiscal land values all converge within a 17% range; pointing to a ~$2B+ annual economy.
💰$210.5M in potential tax contributions to central government (income tax + VAT + $942K/year for Bomberos of Costa Rica), yet Nosara receives only ~$10.8M/year in public investment.
🏘Only 51 out of 1,500 short-term rentals are registered and paying taxes. That's a 96.6% informality rate; representing ~$50M in unreported revenue annually.
🏛Nosara contributes ~$4.1M/year to the Municipality in property tax, business licenses, construction fees, and community services; but receives only $25,500 from that contribution (0.6%). In addition, the Municipality manages $580K of funding for roads from central government transfers to provide a total of $606K/year to Nosara.
🗺Fiscal land values haven't been updated since 2017. Market prices are now 12× higher than assessed values on average. Updating the cadastre alone would multiply property tax revenue from $2.67M to $32.3M; no new laws needed.
